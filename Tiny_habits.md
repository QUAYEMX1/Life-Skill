# Tiny Habits & Atomic Habits – Reflection Assignment

## Question 1
The most interesting idea was starting very small, like doing just 2 push-ups after a daily activity. It shows that consistency matters more than intensity, and small habits are easier to maintain.

## Question 2
B = MAP → Behavior = Motivation × Ability × Prompt

- M (Motivation): Desire to do the habit  
- A (Ability): How easy the habit is  
- P (Prompt): Trigger to perform the habit  

To make habits easier, reduce reliance on motivation, make the habit simple, and attach it to a clear trigger.

## Question 3
Celebration is important because it creates a positive emotion, which helps the brain remember and repeat the behavior. Emotions drive habits, so celebrating reinforces the habit quickly.

## Question 4
The idea that improving by 1% every day leads to huge results over time was most interesting. Small improvements compound and create significant change.

## Question 5
The book focuses on identity-based habits. Instead of focusing on goals, it emphasizes becoming the type of person who performs the habit.

## Question 6
To make a habit easier:
- Start small  
- Reduce friction  
- Make it convenient  
- Use the 2-minute rule  

## Question 7
To make a habit harder:
- Increase friction  
- Remove triggers  
- Make access difficult  
- Add obstacles  

## Question 8
Habit: Studying regularly  

- Make cue obvious: Study after dinner  
- Make it attractive: Start with easy topics  
- Make it easy: Study for 10 minutes  
- Make it satisfying: Track progress  

## Question 9
Habit: Reduce phone usage  

- Make cue invisible: Keep phone away  
- Make it unattractive: Think about wasted time  
- Make it hard: Log out of apps  
- Make it unsatisfying: Track screen time
