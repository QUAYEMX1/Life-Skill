## Question 1
**In this video, what was the most interesting story or idea for you?**

The most interesting idea from BJ Fogg’s Tiny Habits video was the concept of starting extremely small, like doing just 2 push-ups after going to the bathroom. It shows that the size of the habit does not matter initially—what matters is consistency. This completely changes the mindset from “big effort” to “tiny action,” making habits feel achievable instead of overwhelming.

## Question 2
**How can you use B = MAP to make making new habits easier? What are M, A, and P?**

BJ Fogg’s behavior model:
**B = MAP → Behavior = Motivation × Ability × Prompt**

- **M (Motivation):** Your desire to do something
- **A (Ability):** How easy the behavior is
- **P (Prompt):** The trigger that reminds you to act

To make habits easier:
- Don’t rely too much on motivation (it fluctuates)
- Increase ability by making the habit **very easy**
- Use clear prompts (like existing routines)

**Example:**
After brushing my teeth (Prompt), I will do 2 push-ups (Ability - easy), regardless of motivation.

## Question 3
**Why is it important to "Shine" or Celebrate after each successful completion of a habit?**

Celebration is important because it creates a **positive emotional response** in your brain. This feeling reinforces the habit and helps your brain recognize it as something worth repeating.

So when you celebrate (even something small like saying “Good job!”), your brain releases dopamine, which strengthens the habit loop and makes you more likely to repeat the behavior.

## Question 4
**In the 1% Better Every Day video, what was the most interesting idea?**

The most interesting idea was the concept that improving by just **1% every day leads to massive growth over time**. Small daily improvements compound into big results, just like compound interest.

It highlights that:
- Success is not about sudden changes
- It is about **consistent small progress**

## Question 5
**What is Atomic Habits' perspective about Identity?**

Atomic Habits emphasizes **identity-based habits** instead of outcome-based habits.

- Outcome-based: “I want to lose weight”
- Identity-based: “I am a healthy person”

The idea is:
> “Every action you take is a vote for the type of person you want to become.”

When habits align with your identity, they become natural and sustainable.

## Question 6
**How to make a habit easier to do?**

According to Atomic Habits:
- Reduce friction
- Start small
- Make the habit convenient

Ways to make habits easier:
- Break tasks into tiny steps
- Prepare your environment in advance
- Use the “2-minute rule” (start with something that takes 2 minutes)

**Example:**
Instead of “study for 2 hours,” start with “open laptop and read 1 page.”

---

## Question 7
**How to make a habit harder to do?**

To reduce bad habits:
- Increase friction
- Make it inconvenient
- Add obstacles

Ways:
- Remove triggers (hide apps, uninstall distractions)
- Make access difficult
- Add effort required to perform the habit

**Example:**
If you want to reduce phone usage:
- Keep your phone in another room
- Log out of social media apps

## Question 8
**Habit to build: Studying consistently**

### Steps to improve:
- **Make cue obvious:** Study after a fixed trigger (e.g., after dinner)
- **Make it attractive:** Study your favorite subject first
- **Make it easy:** Start with just 10 minutes
- **Make it satisfying:** Track progress or reward yourself

**Plan:**
After dinner, I will study for 10 minutes and mark it on my tracker.

---

## Question 9
**Habit to reduce: Excessive phone usage**

### Steps to reduce:
- **Make cue invisible:** Keep phone away while working
- **Make it unattractive:** Remind yourself of wasted time
- **Make it hard:** Log out or uninstall apps
- **Make it unsatisfying:** Track daily screen time and reflect
